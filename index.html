Scoring Model Calculation

The heat index measures relative thermal distress on a scale of 1 to 10 by mapping meteorological measurements to standard Indian summers:

Raw Score = (Apparent Temp × 0.7) + (Humidity × 0.1) - (Wind Speed × 0.15)

Wind speed acts as a negative factor to account for evaporative cooling, while high apparent temp and humidity compound thermal discomfort. The score maps lower values (~15°C) to 1, and extreme indicators (~48°C) to 10.
